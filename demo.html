The Brief

Bomb Rocks Cairo Neighborhood

Security officials say a large bomb exploded near a national-security building in the Shubra district of Cairo, wounding at least six people. There were no immediate reports of deaths from the incident. The bombing comes amid a wave of attacks this summer

Drought Leads Some Parts of California to Sink

Some land in California's Central Valley is sinking as by as much as 2 in. per month as water is pumped out of the ground to serve the state during its historic drought. California has experienced land sinking for decades but never at such a rapid rate

Uber Hired Drivers With Criminal Past, Says Prosecutors

Uber hired 25 drivers in Los Angeles and San Francisco with criminal records ranging from property crimes, sex offenses and murder, prosecutors said on Wednesday. The comments came after the attorneys announced they were filing a 62-page amended complaint

Britain and France Boost Security at Channel Tunnel

Britain and France are fortifying security around the Channel Tunnel and boosting intelligence efforts against traffickers as they try to dissuade thousands of migrants from trying to make the illegal and sometimes deadly undersea journey to British shores

Dwayne Johnson Slated for Disney’s Jungle Cruise Movie

The wrestler turned action hero is attached to star in Jungle Cruise, Disneys long-gestating movie based on the safari-themed amusement-park ride. Plot details have yet to be revealed for the adventure film, other than that its expected to to have a throwback vibe

Thai Police Identify 2 More Suspects in Bombing

Thai police have identified two more suspects in Monday night's bombing of a crowded Bangkok shrine, citing CCTV footage that led to an arrest warrant for an "unidentified foreign man." A spokesman added the attack was "unlikely to be linked to international terrorism"

3 Firefighters Killed in Washington State Wildfire

A sheriff in north-central Washington state says three firefighters have been killed and three to four more have been injured while battling raging wildfires near the town of Twisp. Washington Governor Jay Inslee said conditions remain extremely dangerous

U.N. Says Yemen on Brink of Famine

The war in Yemen has pushed the country to the brink of famine, with both commercial food imports and aid deliveries held up by the fighting and millions of hungry women and children facing possible starvation, the U.N. said on Wednesday

Longer Work Hours Linked to Stroke and Heart Disease

A new study finds those who work 55 or more hours per week have a 33% increased risk of stroke and 13% greater chance of developing coronary heart disease compared to people who work a standard 35- to 40-hour week

Donald Trump Gains on Hillary Clinton in 2016 Poll

Donald Trump is starting to close the gap in a potential general election matchup with Hillary Clinton, according to a new poll, even amid growing concerns about his electability. He trails Clinton by just six points

Subway's Jared to Admit He Paid for Sex With Minors

Federal prosecutors say longtime Subway pitchman Jared Fogle has agreed to plead guilty to engaging in sex acts with minors and receiving child pornography. He'll pay $1.4 million in restitution to 14 minor victims, each receiving $100,000

How Doctors Cured This Woman’s Brain Cancer

Earlier this year, TIME explored the promise of precision medicine in treating cancer patients. We featured one woman who was taking a drug typically used for melanoma to treat her brain tumor. Now, she's been cured
